1.Arun Khetarpal


Full name – Arun Khetarpal
Birth –14 October 1950
Place of Birth –Pune, Maharashtra
Death – 16 December 1971
Death place – Barapind, Shakargarh Punjab
Age of Death – 21 years old
Occupation – Indian Army (Officers)
Citizenship – Indian
Religion –Hindu
War – Indo Pakistan War 1971, Battle of Basantsar
Awards – PVC 1971 ( paramveer chakra )

Arun Khetarpal was born on 14 October 1950 in Pune, Maharashtra. He had spent his life in many cities apart from Maharashtra since childhood, because his father was in the army, so he used to be transferred from place to place. Arun Khetarpal’s father’s name is Madanlal Khetarpal. His father Madanlal Khetrapal was serving in the Indian Army in the rank of Lieutenant Colonel and then Brigadier.

[Early life]

Arun Khetarpal’s education has been done in many cities since the beginning, because Arun Khetarpal’s father Madanlal Khetarpal was employed in the army, then he was transferred to many cities.. Because of this, Arun Khetarpal’s education has also taken place in many cities. But Arun’s last 5 years of education was at Lawrence School, Sanawar. Arun Khetarpal was a very smart student, he always passed with good marks. Apart from this, he liked to play cricket. In 1968, Arun Khetarpal got the opportunity to join the NDA. His NDA number was 7498/F/38. Arun Khetarpal did his college education from National Defense Academy Khadakwasla and Indian Military Academy Dehradun.


[Arun Khetarpal's Army Career]

The Indian Army career of Arun Khetarpal started in the year 1971. Arun Khetarpal started his military life on 13 June 1971. His military life was only 6 months. Arun Khetrapal was commissioned on 16 December 1971 during the Indo-Pakistani War of 17 Poona Horse under the command of 47th Infantry Brigade of the Indian Army. The 45th Brigade was stationed in the Shakargarh sector during the period of the conflict. In the military life of 6 months, he made a great sacrifice for the country and became immortal for the country.

[Becoming Immortal!!]

On 16 December 1971, during the Battle of Basantar, Lieutenant Khetarpal was in command of a tank troop of the 17 Poona Horse regiment. The regiment was tasked with capturing an enemy position across a canal in the Shakargarh sector of Punjab. Despite facing heavy enemy fire, Khetarpal and his troop managed to cross the canal and engage the enemy tanks. During the battle, Khetarpal's tank was hit by enemy fire, causing it to catch fire and become immobile. Despite being severely wounded, Khetarpal refused to abandon his tank and continued to engage the enemy until he was killed. His gallantry and determination inspired his fellow soldiers and contributed significantly to the success of the operation. However, his sacrifice resulted in his untimely demise at the young age of 21.
